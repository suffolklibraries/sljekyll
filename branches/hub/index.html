Code club

Code Club is a nationwide network of free, volunteer-led coding clubs aimed at children aged 9-11. Volunteers teach programming, encouraging the children to learn while using their imagination in creative projects. Bring your own laptop or use one of our library Chromebooks. Please note tablets are not suitable for this course. Children aged between 6-14 can take part. We ask that a grown-up stays within the library during the sessions. Our first course is fully booked (beginning Saturday 16 January 2016, 2-3pm, for 4 consecutive Saturdays), but you can book a spot for future sessions.

MakerSpace

Situated in the Lab, our MakerSpace is an open, friendly public space to design and create. We offer a range of equipment including two 3D printers, a 3D scanner, two computer coding kits and two professional sewing machines. Find out about our facilities in the MakerSpace. Are you experienced with fashion and textile design, coding or 3D printers? We’re looking for skilled and enthusiastic volunteers to help support our newly built MakerSpace. Contact us at hub@suffolklibraries.co.uk or call 01473 263827 to get involved! Book a machine →
