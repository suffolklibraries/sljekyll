**Many critics draw a distinction between genre and so called 'literary' fiction and discount popular taste. John Nightingale and John Sutherland will discuss whether crime fiction deserves to be taken more seriously and challenge the audience to a crime quiz.**

<img src="/images/featured/featured-john-nightingale.jpg" alt="John Nightingale" class="custom-br-50 mw-40 {% include /c/img-float-right.html %}" />

John Nightingale's latest crime novel <cite>The Appearance of Murder</cite> featuring crime writer David Knight, was chosen by John Sutherland as one of his books of the year in <cite>The Times</cite> in 2015, alongside many literary works. It was also Ian Rankin's pick for his Christmas stocking. John lives and works in London and Suffolk.

<img src="/images/featured/featured-john-sutherland.jpg" alt="John Sutherland" class="custom-br-50 mw-40 {% include /c/img-float-right.html %}" />

John Sutherland has written and thought extensively about popular fiction and loves the puzzles at the heart of the crime genre. He is the author of many books including <cite>Is Heathcliff a Murderer?</cite> and <cite>How to be Well Read</cite>. His latest book is <cite>Orwell's Nose: a pathological biography</cite>, which covers Orwell's year in Southwold. John is the Emeritus Lord Northcliffe Professor of Modern English Literature at University College London.

A book signing will follow this event, with books for sale courtesy of Beccles Books.
