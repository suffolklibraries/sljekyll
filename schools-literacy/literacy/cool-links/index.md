Below are some useful links for ideas about book, reading and literacy – send us your suggestions too!
<h2>The place to start</h2>
Suffolk Libraries <a href="http://suffolklibraries.co.uk/reading-lists-groups-advice">Reading ideas, advice and groups</a>. Also find out about local events, our Business and Innovation Hub, Books on Prescription, our Mental Health service, get free access to online subscription research websites, and more!
<h2>Book Blogs</h2>
There are some super blogs written by passionate book lovers. Here are some of our favourites!
<ul>
	<li><a href="http://bookzone4boys.blogspot.co.uk/">The Book Zone </a>- Aimed at books for boys (but also for girls!)</li>
	<li><a href="http://www.thebookette.co.uk/">The Bookette</a> - Great blog from a school librarian in Essex with reviews and ideas.</li>
	<li><a href="http://www.overflowinglibrary.com/">The Overflowing Library </a>- Wonderful reviews and more written by Kirsty (who lives on the North Norfolk coast). She is also responsible for…</li>
	<li><a href="http://middlegradestrikesback.blogspot.co.uk/">Middle Grade Strikes Back</a> - Who blog about books that would fall under the Middle Grade age bracket (8 - 13 ish if you haven't come across the term).</li>
</ul>
<h2>Young Adult Book Blogs</h2>
<ul>
	<li><a href="http://queenofcontemporary.com/">Queen of Contemporary </a>- Lucy is an advocate of UKYA – young adult fiction written by authors born or currently residing in the UK. An excellent blog to visit daily!</li>
	<li><a href="http://www.yayeahyeah.com/" target="_blank">yayeahyeah</a> - blog that mainly concentrates on YA but occasionally into MG</li>
</ul>
<h2>For Teachers (and anyone else!)</h2>
<ul>
	<li><a href="https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLghL9V9QTN0jTgA1qrhWrBCB_Ln4xlVlB">Thug Notes</a> - Classic Literature. Original Gangster. From plot debriefs to key motifs, Thug Notes Summary &amp; Analysis has you covered with themes, symbols, important quotes, and more.</li>
	<li><a href="http://geoffbarton.co.uk/">Geoff Barton</a> - Geoff is the headteacher of King Edward VI in Bury St Edmunds. He provides quality teaching resources on his brilliant blog.</li>
	<li><a href="http://www.bookbuzz.org.uk/">Bookbuzz </a>- make sure your school is signed up for this annual programme of free/subsidised books for year 7 pupils. Really good resources for promoting reading for pleasure also.</li>
	<li>School Library Pack - and also make sure you're signed up for the school library pack. 4o free books and resources!</li>
</ul>
