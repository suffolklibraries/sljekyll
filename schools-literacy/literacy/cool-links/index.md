Below are some useful links for ideas about book, reading and literacy – send us your suggestions too!

## The place to start

Suffolk Libraries [reading ideas and suggestions](/new-suggestions/). Also find out about local events, our Business and Innovation Hub, Books on Prescription, our Mental Health service, get free access to online subscription research websites, and more!

## Book Blogs

There are some super blogs written by passionate book lovers. Here are some of our favourites!

* [The Book Zone](http://bookzone4boys.blogspot.co.uk/) - Aimed at books for boys (but also for girls!)
* [The Bookette](http://www.thebookette.co.uk/) - Great blog from a school librarian in Essex with reviews and ideas.
* [The Overflowing Library](http://www.overflowinglibrary.com/) - Wonderful reviews and more written by Kirsty (who lives on the North Norfolk coast). She is also responsible for…
* [Middle Grade Strikes Back](http://middlegradestrikesback.blogspot.co.uk/) - Who blog about books that would fall under the Middle Grade age bracket (8 - 13 ish if you haven't come across the term).

## Young Adult Book Blogs

* [Queen of Contemporary](http://queenofcontemporary.com/) - Lucy is an advocate of UKYA – young adult fiction written by authors born or currently residing in the UK. An excellent blog to visit daily!
* [yayeahyeah](http://www.yayeahyeah.com/) - blog that mainly concentrates on YA but occasionally into MG

## For Teachers (and anyone else!)

* [Thug Notes](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLghL9V9QTN0jTgA1qrhWrBCB_Ln4xlVlB) - Classic Literature. Original Gangster. From plot debriefs to key motifs, Thug Notes Summary &amp; Analysis has you covered with themes, symbols, important quotes, and more.
* [Geoff Barton](http://geoffbarton.co.uk/) - Geoff is the headteacher of King Edward VI in Bury St Edmunds. He provides quality teaching resources on his brilliant blog.
* [Bookbuzz](http://www.bookbuzz.org.uk/) - make sure your school is signed up for this annual programme of free/subsidised books for year 7 pupils. Really good resources for promoting reading for pleasure also.
* School Library Pack - and also make sure you're signed up for the school library pack. 40 free books and resources!
